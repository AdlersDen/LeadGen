Handover & Usage Guide

Everything you need to operate the platform — from starting it up to sending your first outreach email.
Version 1.0  ·  July 2026  ·  For internal use by the Adler's Den team

What This App Does

The platform automates B2B lead generation in three stages:

  1. Discover — finds companies near a pincode or inside a business complex (via Google Maps)
  2. Extract — finds verified decision-maker contacts at those companies: HR, Marketing, Admin, Procurement (via Apollo.io)
  3. Outreach — sends personalised emails from marketing@adlersden.com (via Resend)

All data is stored in a Google Sheet you can open and read at any time — no database knowledge needed.

1Starting the App — Do This Every Time

Important: The app has two parts, and they must be started in this order. If you skip Step A, the app will look fine but every button will silently fail or time out.

Step A — Wake up the backend (Render)

The backend server sleeps after 15 minutes of inactivity to save cost. Wake it first:

  1. Open this URL in your browser:
https://adlers-den-leadgen.onrender.com/health
  1. Wait until the page shows: {"status": "ok", "message": "Adler's Den backend is running!"}
  2. This takes 30–90 seconds on first load. Be patient — the server is booting.

Step B — Open the frontend (Vercel)

Once the backend responds, open the app itself:

https://adlersden-leadgen.vercel.app

You'll land on the Dashboard. The app is now ready to use.

2Discover Companies

Where: Sidebar → Discover

Search ModeWhen to UseExample Input
PincodeFind all B2B companies in an area400051 (BKC)
Complex / BuildingTarget one office building or IT parkMaker Chambers, Mindspace Malad

Optional filters:

  • Industry — pick one or more (Event Management, Finance, IT/Tech…). Leave blank to search all industries.
  • Company Tier — keep the default A + B. Tier A = good rating and a real website. Tier B = has a website. Tier C = no website (these can't have contacts extracted, so they're excluded by default).

Click Discover, wait ~20 seconds, and review the company cards. The app automatically filters out residential societies, government offices, schools, clinics, restaurants, and micro-shops — you should only see real businesses.

3Extract Contacts

Where: Sidebar → Contacts → click the Extract Contacts button (top-right)

  1. A panel opens listing companies that haven't been processed yet
  2. Tick the companies you want (or "Select All") — maximum 20 per run
  3. Click Extract for Selected
  4. Wait — roughly 5–8 seconds per company. Keep the tab open while the progress bar runs.

The app finds up to 3 decision-makers per company, prioritising HR, Marketing, Admin and Procurement roles. Every email is verified (MX check) before saving. Contacts appear in the table with role, email, phone, location, LinkedIn and a confidence score.

Tip: Use the Group by Role button next to the search bar to organise the table into sections — HR & People, Marketing & Brand, Admin & Procurement, etc. Amber "(personal)" tags mark Gmail/Yahoo addresses.

4Send Outreach Emails

Where: Sidebar → Outreach

  1. Select the contacts you want to email
  2. Review the pre-filled subject and body — you can edit freely, or use the AI-generated pitch
  3. Click Send

Emails go out from marketing@adlersden.com with the branded Adler's Den template, a "Schedule a Call" button, and the mandatory unsubscribe footer.

Built-in safety rules (you don't need to manage these):
  • 90-day cooldown — the same person can't be emailed twice within 90 days ("In Cooldown" badge)
  • Daily limit: 50 emails/day — protects the sender reputation
  • Unsubscribe blocklist — anyone who opts out is never emailed again

5Monitoring & Data

  • Dashboard — totals for companies, contacts, emails sent, delivery/open rates
  • Companies — every discovered company with tier, domain and extraction status
  • Run History — log of every discovery run with counts and timestamps
  • Google Sheet — the raw database. Open the sheet named test001 from Google Drive. Tabs: Companies, Contacts, Outreach, Runs, Blocklist. You can read anything; avoid editing IDs.

Troubleshooting

ProblemCauseFix
App loads but buttons do nothing / spin forever Backend is asleep Open the /health URL, wait for "ok", then refresh the app
Discovery returns 0 companies Area has no matches, or filter too narrow Remove the industry filter or try a nearby pincode
Extraction finds 0 contacts for a company Company too small for Apollo's database Normal — try larger companies (Tier A first)
"Daily send limit reached" 50 emails already sent today Wait until tomorrow, or raise DAILY_SEND_LIMIT on Render
"Already contacted within 90 days" Cooldown protection Working as intended — pick other contacts
Recipient says email not received Possibly in their spam/quarantine Check Resend dashboard → Logs for the delivery status
If something breaks that this table doesn't cover: check Render → your service → Logs. Every error the backend hits is written there with a timestamp. Screenshot the log line before asking for help.
